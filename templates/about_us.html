About Us

Nestled in rural Co. Limerick, in the middle of horse country, a stones throw away from the world renowned Scarteen Hunt, and just up the road from the birthplace of Steeplechasing itself, is a Lady determined to uphold one of Irelands greatest natural resources, traditional Irish Sporthorse bloodlines. Fiona Hickey is carrying on the family tradition entrusted to her by her father Denis, who was a prolific breeder in his day with numerous Dublin Horse Show winners.

A “Hickey horse”, as they are still known, is known for being a good looking, brave & athletic horse.

Fiona sticks rigidly to time honoured methods of producing horses. They are all foaled out as nature intended, under her supervision and run-in herds on the farm until they are 3 years of age. “There is something lovely about just watching them in the field...just being horses !”.

Past to present

Back in the olden days, manys the local farmer had only one horse and they would borrow a Hickey horse to plough their fields or mow their meadows. Horses from Ballyneety have gone on to careers as Field Hunters, Show Hunters, as Eventers, Showjumpers, Equitation horses & more. Several have gone on to stellar careers in the upper echelons of sport, having had their names changed. Deeply appreciative of the growing recognition that Ballyneety horses are receiving, everyone at Ballyneety Stud Farm can be very proud and also excited about the next generation of quality youngsters coming on in the fields.

Fiona has a wonderful sense of humour and tells a great story of a lady she met at a show. This lady introduced herself and proclaimed that she had a Ballyneety horse. Fiona said to her, “sure a bird never flew on one wing!”. That same lady has since purchased 5 Ballyneety horses at Ballyneety. Such is the quality available in terms of the wonderful warm welcome, the horses and the services rendered that people keep returning to Fiona and all at Ballyneety Stud farm.
